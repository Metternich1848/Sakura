花見 (はなみ)

Commonly known as Hanami(はなみ/花見）in Japanese, cherry blossom festivals are significant traditions that are held all over Japan every spring. Thousands of people flock to parks and gardens to enjoy the fleeting beauty of Sakura (さくら/桜). Friends and families huddle down under blooming cherry trees with homemade food and drinks.

姫路城(ひめじじょう)

Crowned as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO, Himeji Castle is famous for its elegant white appearance and impressive size. Regarded as a national treasure, Himeji Castle is the most well-preserved example of a medieval fortress architecture in all of Japan. Over a thousand cherry trees around the castle bloom spectacularly every year from late March to mid-April, making the celebrated castle one of the best spots in Japan to appreciate cherry blossoms in spring.
